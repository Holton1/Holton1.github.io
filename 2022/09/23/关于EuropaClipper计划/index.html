关于EuropaClipper计划

NASA planned to send a spacecraft to flyby Europa, a moon of Jupyter. Europa is the second biggest moon of Jupyter, in a similar size of Earth moon but covered by ice, and is one of the Galilean moon contains a lot of water. Previous space missions have some information about Europa. The vovyars have flied across Europa and have some photos but in low resolutions. The Juno spacecraft visited Jupyter and get more information of the surface of Europa from 2005-2013. Juno have high resolution photos of the Europa surface, but still leaves many questions unclear. But previous research points to a remarkable conclusion: A subsurface layer of liquid water may exists under Europa’s icy surface layer, which may be habitable for microorganisms prohibit to those on terristrial surface. The new mission of visiting Europa hopefully can help understand the habitability of Europa subsurface layer.

The Europa Clipper will be launched in mid-2020. If it follows Venus-Earth-Earth orbit, it takes around 6 years to arrive Jupyter. If it goes along a direct orbit the period can be less than 3.5 years. The Europa Clipper will carrys multiple sensors to determine the atmospheric constitutions. It will fly along orbits around Jupyter and fly by Europa for 30 times in 3 years, and get the full surface scan. It can capture some samples if encountering eruptions.
